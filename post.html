How to Prepare Your Home for Sale

March 15, 2025 • by Lizzie Buras, REALTOR® • 8 min read
Gulf Coast Real Estate | Licensed in Louisiana

A well-staged home can attract more buyers and sell faster

Selling your home can be both exciting and stressful. After years of making memories in your space, it's time to help someone else envision it as their future home. The key to a successful sale often lies in the preparation. With my experience helping families in Houma buy and sell homes through Gulf Coast Real Estate, I've learned what works – and what doesn't – when it comes to preparing your home for the market.

1. Start with Curb Appeal

First impressions matter, and your home's exterior is the first thing potential buyers see. In Louisiana's climate, homes can face unique challenges from humidity, storms, and intense sun. Here's how to make your home's exterior shine:

  • Pressure wash everything: Siding, sidewalks, driveways, and porches. Louisiana's humidity can cause mildew and dirt buildup.
  • Refresh your landscaping: Trim overgrown bushes, plant colorful flowers, and ensure your lawn is well-maintained.
  • Update your front door: A fresh coat of paint in a welcoming color can make a huge difference.
  • Check your roof and gutters: Ensure they're clean and in good repair – buyers notice these details.

💡 Pro Tip from Lizzie

"In Houma, I always recommend power washing your home's exterior about 2-3 weeks before listing. Our humid climate can make even newer homes look tired, but a good cleaning can take years off your home's appearance!"

2. Declutter and Depersonalize

This step is often the hardest for sellers, but it's crucial for helping buyers envision themselves in your space. You want to create a blank canvas that allows their imagination to run wild.

  • Remove personal photos and memorabilia: While these items make your house feel like home to you, they can distract buyers.
  • Clear countertops and surfaces: Keep only essential items visible. Store everything else.
  • Reduce furniture: Less furniture makes rooms appear larger and more open.
  • Organize closets and storage: Buyers will look, and organized spaces suggest the home has adequate storage.

3. Make Necessary Repairs

Small issues can become big red flags for buyers. Address these common problems before listing:

  • Fix leaky faucets and running toilets: These suggest poor maintenance to buyers.
  • Replace burnt-out light bulbs: Ensure every light fixture works properly.
  • Repair holes in walls: Patch and paint any damage from hanging pictures or furniture.
  • Address any water damage: This is especially important in Louisiana's humid climate.
  • Service your HVAC system: A well-maintained system is crucial for Louisiana buyers.

4. Paint for Maximum Appeal

Fresh paint is one of the most cost-effective ways to update your home. Stick to neutral colors that appeal to the widest range of buyers:

  • Choose warm, neutral tones: Light grays, beiges, and off-whites work well.
  • Paint high-traffic areas: Hallways, kitchens, and bathrooms should look fresh.
  • Don't forget the trim: Fresh white trim makes everything look crisp and clean.
  • Consider accent walls sparingly: If you use them, keep them subtle and tasteful.

🎨 Color Recommendations for Houma Homes

Based on what I've seen work well in our local market, here are some paint colors that appeal to Houma buyers:

  • Sherwin Williams "Accessible Beige" - warm and welcoming
  • Benjamin Moore "Classic Gray" - sophisticated and timeless
  • Behr "Swiss Coffee" - crisp white with warmth

5. Stage for Success

Staging helps buyers visualize how they'll use each space. You don't need to hire a professional – simple staging can be very effective:

  • Arrange furniture to show flow: Create clear pathways through rooms.
  • Add fresh flowers or plants: They bring life and color to spaces.
  • Use lighting strategically: Open blinds, turn on lamps, and let in natural light.
  • Set the dining table: This helps buyers envision entertaining.
  • Make beds and fluff pillows: Bedrooms should feel like retreats.

6. Don't Forget the Details

Small touches can make a big difference in how buyers perceive your home:

  • Deep clean everything: Consider hiring professionals for carpets and windows.
  • Replace outdated fixtures: Updated light fixtures and cabinet hardware make a big impact.
  • Add subtle scents: Light candles or use diffusers with neutral scents like vanilla or clean linen.
  • Keep temperature comfortable: Ensure your home feels comfortable during showings.
  • Hide pet evidence: Remove food bowls, toys, and ensure there are no odors.

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

In my experience with Gulf Coast Real Estate, here are the most common mistakes sellers make when preparing their homes:

  • Over-improving for the neighborhood
  • Ignoring minor repairs that buyers notice
  • Using too many bold colors or personal decorating choices
  • Forgetting to clean out closets and storage areas
  • Not addressing odors (pets, cooking, smoking)

7. Price It Right from the Start

Even the most beautifully prepared home won't sell if it's priced incorrectly. Work with your REALTOR® to analyze recent sales of comparable homes in your neighborhood. In Houma's market, pricing strategy can make or break a sale.

Remember, the first two weeks on the market are crucial. Homes that are priced right from the beginning generate more interest, more showings, and often sell faster and for more money than homes that start too high and have to reduce their price later.

Ready to Sell Your Houma Home?

Preparing your home for sale takes time and effort, but the results speak for themselves. A well-prepared home typically sells faster and for more money than one that's not properly staged and priced.
